# Claim Categories

Use this taxonomy when extracting verifiable claims from an article. Each claim should be assigned one category.

## Hard Facts

These are definitively true or false. Prioritize these for research.

### Pricing
Specific dollar amounts, plan tiers, free tier limits, billing frequency.
- "The Pro plan costs $20/month"
- "They offer a free tier with 1,000 API calls"
- "Enterprise pricing starts at $500/seat/year"

### Features & Capabilities
What a product can or cannot do, integrations, supported platforms, output types.
- "Notion supports real-time collaboration"
- "The API does not support batch operations"
- "It runs on macOS, Linux, and Windows"

### Version & Release
Version numbers, release dates, what version introduced a feature.
- "React 18 introduced concurrent rendering"
- "This feature shipped in v3.2"
- "The SDK requires Node 18 or higher"

### Dates & Timeline
When events happened, founding dates, launch dates, milestones.
- "The company was founded in 2019"
- "They launched their API in March 2024"
- "The feature has been available since May 2025"

### Statistics
Market share, usage numbers, performance benchmarks, survey results.
- "70% of developers use VS Code"
- "The average response time is 200ms"
- "Over 10 million users have signed up"

### Architecture & Technical
How something works under the hood, execution model, infrastructure, security model.
- "It uses a microservices architecture"
- "Data is encrypted at rest with AES-256"
- "Tasks run inside a sandboxed virtual machine"

### Availability & Status
Whether something is available, deprecated, in beta, generally available, or sunset.
- "This feature is currently in research preview"
- "The legacy API was deprecated in 2024"
- "Available on Pro and Max plans"

### Company & Organization
Ownership, team size, funding, partnerships, acquisitions.
- "They raised a $50M Series B"
- "The project is maintained by the Apache Foundation"
- "Acquired by Google in 2023"

## Soft Claims

These are subjective or directional. Research with lower priority, but flag if evidence suggests they no longer hold.

### Comparison Claims
Relative statements between products or approaches.
- "Tool A is faster than Tool B for large datasets"
- "Framework X has a gentler learning curve than Y"
- "Cowork produces better-formatted documents than Code"

### Opinion & Recommendation
Subjective assessments, recommendations, predictions.
- "This is the best approach for small teams"
- "Most marketers prefer a visual editor"
- "The learning curve is steeper but worth it"
